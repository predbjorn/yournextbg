Setup

  1. Board to the middle. Exhibition marker on clock space 1; the three Lead medallions beside it, unowned.
  2. Place the Microscope & Engraver tokens on their Study spots.
  3. Shuffle the engine deck; deal 6 face-up to the Field row. Deck on its space.
  4. Shuffle the Tactic deck near the board.
  5. Each player takes a Faction, their starter set, 3 Specimens, draws 1 Tactic, and sets their Standing marker to 0.
  6. Randomly set turn order; place priority pawns on the 1–4 track. All begin at 0 Standing.

Where things go

On the board: the Field draw deck + its 6-card row + priority track; the Study benches + the two prime spots; the Hall’s five nodes; the Exhibition clock and the Standing track (one marker each).

The bank suggested — Specimens & Findings in a shared pile beside the board. Spent and spoiled tokens go back here.

Your stores & tableau suggested — keep your Specimens/Findings in your area; build your cards into three rows Field / Study / Hall so each card’s plane is obvious. Findings over the cap of 3 spoil to the bank at end of turn.

Discards suggested — a pile beside the engine deck and another beside the Tactic deck.

Claims at the Hall — a disc on a node ring. Open: strength shown (a number token or die). Lodged: face-down, strength hidden under it suggested — a chit or a jotted note. A discredited claim returns to its owner; spent Findings are simply lost.

Medallions when earned suggested — hand the medallion to its holder, kept in view, so a two-medallion threat (and the all-three knockout) is obvious.
