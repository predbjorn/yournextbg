Cryptids, Trophies & Lanes

A natural-history engine-builder for 2–4 players
· · ✦ · ·
Win Condition. Be the first player to hold all three trophy medallions simultaneously: Collector (most species named), Systematist (most complete sets), and Sensationalist (most confirmed dubious species). Trophies are recalculated after every action — they pass hands fluidly.

1 · Overview

Each player controls a Victorian natural-history society. Over successive rounds you build an engine across four lanes — Expedition, Field, Study, Hall — to gather Specimens, convert them to Findings, and use Findings to name species in the market. Named species score trophy leads. Hold all three leads at once and you win.

The game also features a dubious species sub-game: cryptids and legendary creatures can be added to evidence bags via Sightings and then verified. Genuine vindications score the Sensationalist trophy; exposed hoaxes disgrace the top contributor.

A shared Clock counts down to end-of-game if no knockout is achieved. When the clock fills, all scores are tallied and the player with the most medallions wins.

2 · Components (demo version)

Cards (31 engine cards)

  • 7 Expedition cards (tiers 1–3)
  • 8 Field cards (tiers 1–3)
  • 8 Study cards (tiers 1–3)
  • 8 Hall cards (tiers 1–3)

Other

  • 27 species cards (8 common, 7 rare, 6 sensational, 6 dubious/legendary)
  • 5 sets: Winged, Terrestrial, Botanical, Legendary, Anomalous
  • Discovery deck: 4 Specimen · 2 Finding · 2 Sighting · 3 Hazard
  • 8 tactic cards (×2 copies each)
  • 5 factions

Resources tracked per player

Specimens — raw material Findings — processed evidence (capped) Standing — prestige / tiebreaker Sightings — tokens for dubious bags

3 · Setup

  1. Each player chooses a faction. Turn order is randomised.
  2. Each player starts with 3 Specimens, 2 Sightings, and 1 tactic card in hand.
  3. Each player starts with three starter cards already in their tableau:
    • Naturalist's Pack (Expedition) — free mount, 1 draw
    • Field Journal (Study) — converts 2 Spec → 1 Finding (max 4)
    • Field Report (Hall) — naming card, chains to Field after use
  4. Shuffle the engine deck (31 cards minus starters) and deal 5 cards face-up as the Draft Row.
  5. Shuffle all 27 species and deal 6 face-up as the Species Market. Dubious species arrive with a secret evidence bag sealed with a truth token (Genuine or Hoax, ~67%/33%).
  6. Shuffle the Discovery deck (11 tokens, reshuffled if exhausted).
  7. Set the Clock to 0 (closes at 12 by default).

4 · Round Structure

Play proceeds in rounds. Each round every player takes one full turn in priority order. After all players have acted the round ends; leads are recomputed and the next round begins.

Your Turn

On your turn you may take any number of actions until you choose to stop or have no legal actions remaining. Actions may be chained (see §6). End your turn explicitly with Stop / End Turn.

At the start of your turn:

  • Your Findings decay to 3 (the base cap) unless you hold a card with Never Decay (Darkroom).
  • Your typed field access resets. You must re-activate field cards to unlock field prerequisites again this turn.
Lane Leads. Each lane has a current leader (the player scoring highest by that lane's metric). Lane leads act as a tiebreaker for trophies and as a cost/discount signal in chaining. They update after every action, not just at end-of-turn.

5 · The Four Actions

Expedition Lane

Mount an Expedition

Pay the card's Mount Cost in Specimens. The expedition begins: draw tokens from the Discovery deck equal to the card's Draws value, resolving each in order.

Discovery Tokens

  • Specimen — banked; converts to +1 Specimen on banking
  • Finding — banked; converts to +1 Finding on banking
  • Sighting — banked; converts to +1 Sighting on banking
  • Hazard — if unprotected, bust: all banked tokens lost, turn ends immediately

After the initial draws, if the expedition is still active you may:

  • Press Deeper — draw one more token, resolve it, repeat if you wish
  • Bank & Return — collect all banked tokens as resources; triggers chain if the card has a trigger

Hazard protection (Deep Expedition: 1, Royal Commission: 2) absorbs one Hazard per shield, then is expended. The Expeditioner faction ignores the first Hazard for free each turn.

Field Lane

Activate a Field Card

No cost. Gain Specimens as listed. Some cards also unlock a typed field (e.g. botanical, wetlands) which is required to name certain species in the Hall this turn.

  • The Collector faction gains +1 Specimen on every Field action.
  • specPerField cards (Local Guide, Patronage Contract) give a per-activation bonus once.
  • specWhileLead cards (Network of Informants) give a bonus only if you currently hold the Field lead.
  • Cards with a trigger automatically chain into the Study lane after resolving.
Study Lane

Convert via a Study Card

Spend Specimens in batches of convIn to produce batches of convOut Findings, up to convMaxIn Specimens total.

Findings are capped at your Findings Cap (base 3; each Photography Kit or Reference Library in your study adds +1). Overflow is lost unless you hold Darkroom (Never Decay).

If you activate Study as a chain and you are not the Study lead, you must first spend 1 Specimen as a toll. If you are the Study lead, no toll.

The Comparative Method doubles output if you hold the Study lead.

Hall Lane

Name a Species (Hall Card)

Spend Findings equal to the species' cost. The species must be in the market. You must have activated the required typed field cards earlier this turn (unless you are the Sensationalist and the species is dubious).

Your Strength = Findings spent + card's strBonus.

Uncontested Naming

Add the species to the named list under your name. Some Hall cards grant +1 Standing on naming.

Challenging a Rival's Species

If the species is already named by another player, pay the cost and compare strengths:

  • Your strength > defender's: you wrest the name; defender loses one named count.
  • Tie: both papers are discredited; species becomes unclaimed.
  • Your strength < defender's: defender gains +1 Standing; you still pay the cost.

If you are chaining into Hall and are not the Hall lead, pay 1 Finding toll first.

Named species leave the market and are replaced. The Systematist faction gets +1 strength when naming species with 2+ field requirements.

6 · Chaining

Several cards carry a trigger which grants a free chain into another lane immediately after the action resolves. You are not required to use the chain.

Chain toll (Study & Hall only). When entering Study or Hall via a chain, you pay 1 Specimen or 1 Finding respectively unless you currently hold that lane's lead. The toll is paid once per chain entry, not once per action within that chain.

Cards that trigger chains

→ Field

  • Field Report → Field
  • Illustrated Plate → Field
  • Definitive Monograph → Field
  • Sensational Report → Field
  • Ghost Map → Field

→ Study

  • Botanical Survey → Study
  • Mountain Expedition (card) → Study
  • Fossil Dig → Study

→ Hall

  • Pressing Board → Hall
  • Taxonomy Manual → Hall
  • Reference Library → Hall
  • Darkroom → Hall

7 · Drafting Engine Cards

At any point during your turn you may draft one or more cards from the five-card Draft Row by paying each card's cost in Specimens. The card immediately enters the appropriate lane of your tableau. A replacement card is drawn from the engine deck for each card taken.

Tier 1 cards are free. Tier 2 cards cost 2–3. Tier 3 cards cost 4–5.

8 · Dubious Species & Fraud

Six species are marked Dubious (Kraken, Feejee Mermaid, Vegetable Lamb, Sea Serpent, Jackalope, Yeti). Each is sealed with a secret truth token — Genuine (~67% of the time) or Hoax (~33%).

Sightings

Spend 1 Sighting token to add an anonymous sighting to a dubious species' evidence bag. Sightings are tracked per player.

Verification

Once a bag holds ≥ 2 sightings, any player may spend an action to Call Verification.

  • The truth token is revealed.
  • The player with the most sightings in the bag is identified as the top contributor.
Genuine! Top contributor gains +4 Standing and +1 Confirmed Dubious (scores the Sensationalist trophy). If they haven't already named the species, it is named for them at strength 0.
Hoax! Top contributor loses Standing equal to their sighting count (max −4) and loses their name claim on that species if they held one.

The Sensationalist faction may name dubious species without field prerequisites and pays 1 fewer Finding per naming.

9 · Trophies & Win Condition

🏆 Collector

Most species named. Tiebreak: Standing.

🏆 Systematist

Most complete sets. Tiebreak: named count.

🏆 Sensationalist

Most confirmed-genuine dubious species. Tiebreak: Standing.

All three trophies are recalculated after every action. A player who holds all three simultaneously wins immediately (knockout). If the Clock fills before a knockout, the player with the most trophies wins; further tiebreaks use Standing.

Species Sets & Set Bonuses

There are five sets. Completing a set grants a one-time bonus:

  • Winged — +2 Findings
  • Terrestrial — +3 Specimens
  • Botanical — +2 Standing
  • Legendary — +1 Confirmed Dubious (free!)
  • Anomalous — draw 1 Tactic card

10 · The Clock

The shared Clock advances by 1 at the start of each round. It closes at 12 (adjustable). When the Clock closes, the game ends immediately after the current round is fully resolved — no mid-round stops.

Sensational Report (Hall tier 3) advances the Clock by 1 when played for a naming. Some factions or tactics may also interact with the Clock in full rules.

14 · Complete Turn Example

3-player game, Round 4. Players: The Collector (you), The Systematist, The Expeditioner. The Lyrebird is in the market (cost 2 Findings; needs winged and tropical).

Your state at turn start
Specimens 5 Findings 1 (cap 3) Standing 2 Sightings 1
Tableau: Exp — Naturalist's Pack, Survey Permit · Field — Specimen Hunt, Botanical Survey, Marsh Survey · Study — Field Journal · Hall — Field Report
Draft Row: Taxonomy Manual (T2, cost 2) · Network of Informants (T2, cost 2) · Pressing Board (T1, free) · Fossil Dig (T3, cost 4) · Field Compass (T1, free)
Market: House Sparrow · Reed Warbler · Axolotl · Lyrebird · Jackalope(?) · Yeti(?)
Action 1Draft · Pressing Board
→

Pressing Board is free (T1, cost 0). You take it from the Draft Row into your Study lane. A replacement card is drawn: Illustrated Plate appears.

Specimens 5 → 5 (no cost). Study tableau now has Field Journal + Pressing Board.

Action 2Field — Botanical Survey
→

Activate Botanical Survey (T1, free). Base gain: +2 Specimens. You are The Collector, so faction ability adds +1 more. Botanical Survey also unlocks the botanical typed field. The card has a trigger → Study.

Specimens 5 + 3 = 8. Typed fields: {botanical}. Auto-chain into Study opens.

Action 2 chainStudy — Pressing Board
→

You are entering Study via the chain from Botanical Survey. Are you the Study lead? No — check: Study lead = player who converted the most Specimens last turn; it's The Systematist. So you pay the 1 Specimen toll first.

You activate Pressing Board: converts in batches of 3 Spec → 1 Finding, max 3 Spec. You have 7 Specimens after the toll. One full batch: spend 3 → gain 1 Finding. The card triggers → Hall.

Specimens 8 − 1 (toll) − 3 (convert) = 4. Findings 1 + 1 = 2. Chain → Hall opens.

Action 2 chain (cont.)Hall — Field Report → name House Sparrow
→

You are entering Hall via the Study chain. Are you the Hall lead? Yes — you currently hold the Collector trophy (1 named species vs 0 for others). No toll.

You use Field Report to name House Sparrow. Cost: 1 Finding. House Sparrow needs the winged typed field. Do you have winged access? No — you only have botanical from Botanical Survey. House Sparrow is blocked. You scan other affordable options: Axolotl (Rare, needs wetlands) — also blocked. Common Fern would need botanical ✓, but it's not in the market today. No naming is possible this chain entry.

However, Field Report's trigger is → Field. That chain is still granted even if you don't name — you simply skip the Hall action but the chain fires.

Findings stay at 2. Chain → Field fires.

Action 2 chain (cont.)Field — Marsh Survey
→

You are in a Field chain triggered by Field Report. Activate Marsh Survey: +4 Specimens + 1 Collector bonus = +5. Marsh Survey also unlocks wetlands typed field.

Specimens 4 + 5 = 9. Typed fields: {botanical, wetlands}. No further trigger.

Action 3Study — Field Journal
→

Back to free actions. Activate Field Journal: converts in batches of 2 Spec → 1 Finding, max 4 Spec. You have 9 Specimens, Findings cap is 3. You already have 2 Findings — only 1 more fits before cap. One batch: spend 2 → gain 1 Finding = 3 Findings (cap hit).

Specimens 9 − 2 = 7. Findings 2 + 1 = 3 (cap). No trigger.

Action 4Hall — name Axolotl
→

Use Field Report again (Hall cards can be reused each turn — they are permanent engine cards, not discards). Name Axolotl (Rare, needs wetlands). You have wetlands access from Marsh Survey ✓. Cost: 2 Findings. No existing claim. Strength = 2 (cost) + 0 (strBonus) = 2. Named uncontested.

Field Report triggers → Field.

Findings 3 − 2 = 1. namedCount 1 → 2. Collector trophy lead strengthened. Chain → Field.

Action 4 chainField — Specimen Hunt
→

Activate Specimen Hunt: +2 Specimens + 1 Collector bonus = +3.

Specimens 7 + 3 = 10. No trigger.

Action 5Expedition — Survey Permit
→

Mount Survey Permit (mount cost 2, draws 2). Pay 2 Specimens. Draw two Discovery tokens from the deck.

Draw 1: Finding → banked.

Draw 2: Specimen → banked.

Expedition still active. You decide to Press Deeper once.

Draw 3: Hazard! Survey Permit has 0 protection. You are not the Expeditioner. Bust — all three banked tokens are lost. Expedition ends immediately.

Specimens 10 − 2 = 8. No tokens collected. Expedition ends.

Action 6Stop — End Turn
→

You end your turn. Leads are recomputed.

Specimens 8 Findings 1 Standing 2 Sightings 1 Named 2

Collector trophy: you hold it (2 named). Systematist trophy: you have 0 complete sets. Sensationalist: 0 confirmed dubious. Trophy count: 1 of 3 — not a knockout yet.

What to build next: To push toward Systematist you need a second named species in the same set. Axolotl is Anomalous — name another Anomalous species (Lyrebird needs tropical; draft Mountain Expedition to unlock it). For Sensationalist: add Sightings to Jackalope's bag and call a Verification. A winning path runs: draft Mountain Expedition (Field) → tropical access → name Lyrebird (Winged+Anomalous) → unlock Systematist lead.
Cryptids, Trophies & Lanes Quick Reference

Quick Reference

Turn skeleton

  • Findings decay to cap (3 base) at start
  • Typed field access resets at start
  • Take any number of actions in any order
  • Draft any time (pay Specimens)
  • End turn explicitly with Stop

Chain tolls

  • Study chain (non-lead): pay 1 Specimen
  • Hall chain (non-lead): pay 1 Finding
  • Field chain: no toll
  • Expedition: no chain in/out (overlay)

Discovery deck odds

  • 4/11 Specimen (36%)
  • 2/11 Finding (18%)
  • 2/11 Sighting (18%)
  • 3/11 Hazard (27%)
  • Reshuffled when empty

Trophy criteria

  • Collector — most named. TB: Standing
  • Systematist — most complete sets. TB: named count
  • Sensationalist — most confirmed-genuine dubious. TB: Standing

Naming strength

  • Strength = Findings spent + card strBonus
  • Systematist: +1 if 2+ fields required
  • Win challenge: strength > defender
  • Tie: both lose; species unclaimed
  • Lose challenge: defender +1 Standing

Verification

  • Requires ≥ 2 sightings in bag
  • Genuine → top contributor +4 Stand, +1 dubious confirmed
  • Hoax → top contributor −sighting count Standing (max −4), loses name claim

Set bonuses (one-time)

  • Winged → +2 Findings
  • Terrestrial → +3 Specimens
  • Botanical → +2 Standing
  • Legendary → +1 Confirmed Dubious
  • Anomalous → draw 1 Tactic

Clock

  • +1 each round at round start
  • Closes at 12 → end of that round
  • Sensational Report: +1 Clock per naming use
  • Knockout wins override Clock
