First in the Field

A game of discovery, scholarship & cutthroat priority among rival naturalists
❖
The Grand Exhibition approaches. Outfit expeditions, classify your finds, and race your rivals to name the new species — for at the Society’s Hall, to be scooped is to be ruined. Build the cleverest engine of discovery, and stand first in the field.
2–4 naturalists · 45–75 minutes · ages 12+
First Draft — for playtest
First in the FieldThe Premise · Components

The Premise

You are a naturalist of the age of wonder, vying to be remembered. The world’s cabinets are filling with strange beetles, ghost orchids and impossible birds, and whoever describes a creature first, and correctly, wins their name upon it forever. Glory is finite: every discovery one rival claims is one you cannot.

The game plays across three bands of the common board, each with its own temperature. The Field is cold and patient — you draft cards and build a discovery engine, untroubled. The Study is warmer — you refine specimens into findings, jostling for the few prime instruments. The Society’s Hall is hot — rival claims clash, sealed and revealed, and reputations are made or broken.

The Goal

You strive to lead the three bands. Each band awards a Lead medallion to whoever best fulfils its condition; the medallion moves the instant another overtakes it.

  • At the Exhibition’s close — the naturalist holding the most Lead medallions wins.
  • Outright — if ever you hold all three medallions at the start of a round, the table cannot stop you in time: you win at once.

There are no victory points to tally. Standing is everything.

Components (provisional)

  • 1 common board — the Field, the Study and the Society’s Hall, the Exhibition clock and the Standing track.
  • ~50 engine cards — Field, Study and Hall cards in three tiers (the deck that feeds the Field row).
  • ~16 Tactic cards — one-shot tricks, held hidden in hand.
  • 5 Faction cards — one asymmetric power each.
  • Resource tokens — Specimens, Findings, Standing markers.
  • 3 Lead medallions (Field, Study, Hall) · 1 Exhibition marker · 2 Prime Tool tokens (Microscope, Engraver).
  • Per player: a set of claim markers (for the Hall), a priority pawn, and a player colour.

First in the FieldSetup · Key Concepts

Setup

  1. Place the board in the middle. Put the Exhibition marker on clock space 1, and set the three Lead medallions beside the board, unowned.
  2. Place the two Prime Tool tokens (Microscope, Engraver) on their spots in the Study.
  3. Shuffle the engine deck and deal 6 cards face-up to the Field row. Shuffle the Tactic deck nearby.
  4. Each player chooses (or draws) a Faction card and takes their three starting cards — one each of Collecting Trip, Field Notes, Present a Paper — into their tableau, plus 3 Specimens and 1 Tactic in hand.
  5. Randomly set the starting priority order; place priority pawns on the track. All begin at 0 Standing.

Key Concepts

The three currencies

Discovery refines upward, and each step keeps less well than the last — how well a currency stores is how hot its band runs.

  • Specimens — raw finds. Stockpile freely.
  • Findings — prepared work. Cap 3; any above your cap spoil at the end of your turn.
  • Acclaim — the strength of a claim. Never stored; spent the instant it is made.

Your tableau & your hand

Cards you build sit permanently in your tableau, sorted into Field / Study / Hall rows — your engine, which no rival may destroy. Tactics stay hidden in hand until played.

The Leads (flow, not hoard)

  • Field — the largest collection (highest total tier of your built Field cards).
  • Study — the highest throughput (most Findings you produced on your last turn).
  • Hall — the most claims standing (nodes you hold).

Ties leave a medallion unowned until broken.

Standing & tolls

Standing is your tempo. It sets priority each round, and may be spent — 1 Standing to waive a toll, or to improve one conversion by one step for a turn.

A toll is paid whenever a chain passes through a band you do not lead: +1 of that band’s input currency (Specimens to cross the Study, Findings to cross the Hall).

First in the FieldA Turn · Chains

Anatomy of a Turn

Play proceeds in rounds; in each round every naturalist takes one turn, in priority order. On your turn you take one action in one band — then end your turn.

BandYour one action
FieldSurvey — take a face-up card into your tableau, paying its build cost in Specimens (then refill the row) — or activate a built Field card to gain Specimens.
StudyActivate a built Study card to convert Specimens → Findings at its ratio.
HallActivate a built Hall card to Present or Lodge a claim (see The Hall).

The card you activate resolves its Activate line; its Passives are always in effect. If it bears a Trigger (→ Band), you may immediately take a free chained action in that band.

Ending your turn: any Findings above your cap spoil; note your throughput for the Study lead; pass priority to the next naturalist.

Chains

A Trigger hands you a free action in the band it points to — but you must still pay that step’s fuel (the currency it consumes) and any toll for crossing a band you do not lead. That step’s own Trigger may chain onward, and so on, until the fuel runs dry, a step fails, or you choose to stop.

  • Chains generally run down the stack: Field → Study → Hall.
  • A successful Hall claim with a → Field trigger loops back to the top — the spoils of a discovery funding the next.
  • A Hall step that is repelled ends the chain at once.

❖ A discovery, start to finish

You spend your action to activate Expedition Upriver (Field): +4 . Its →Study trigger fires.

Free Study step — Classify: convert 4 → 4 (you lead the Study, so no toll). Its →Hall trigger fires.

Free Hall step — Present a Paper at Entomology (empty): a claim of strength 3. It stands. The node’s Cache perk lets you draw a specimen, and the card’s →Field trigger could begin another lap — if you have the fuel. One action; a whole discovery.

First in the FieldThe Field · The Study

The Field cold · draft

The Field is where you build. The only contact here is the race to claim a card first — no rival may ever take or spoil what you have already built. Setbacks are missed chances, never losses.

Surveying (drafting)

The Field offers 6 face-up cards. To Survey, take one into your tableau and pay its build cost in Specimens (Tier I = 0, II = 2, III = 4–5). Then slide the row up and refill from the deck.

Collecting

Instead of Surveying, you may activate a built Field card for its Specimens (and any chain).

Priority

At the start of each round, set turn order by Standing, highest first (ties keep the previous order). The Field lead — the largest collection — is the steady reward for the patient drafter.

The Study warm · work

The Study turns raw finds into scholarship. It runs warmer than the Field for one reason: the prime instruments are few, and only their holder may use them.

Converting

Activate a built Study card to convert Specimens → Findings at its printed ratio (the base is 2:1; better cards reach 1:1). Remember: Findings spoil above your cap of 3 at turn’s end — refine to spend, not to hoard.

Prime tools (the friction)

The Microscope and Engraver are exclusive spots. The first naturalist to use a card that requires one places their disc there and holds the tool until the end of the round; while held, no rival may run a card needing that tool. This is the Study’s only aggression — denial, never destruction.

The Study lead

Awarded for throughput — the most Findings produced on your last turn. It rewards running the engine, not sitting on a pile, which keeps the game moving.

First in the FieldThe Society’s Hall

The Society’s Hall hot · contest

Here scholarship becomes reputation. The Hall holds five fields of study, each a node granting a perk to whoever holds it. To present is to stake a claim; to challenge is to risk ruin.

Two ways to act

  • Present — spend Findings to make an open claim of strength S = Findings spent (capped by the card). Aim it at a node.
  • Lodge a Claim — place a face-down sealed claim of strength = Findings +1 on an empty node or one you hold. Its strength is hidden — this is your garrison, and your bluff.

Present onto an empty node (or one you already hold) and your claim simply stands. Present onto a node a rival holds, and you must challenge it.

Challenge — reveal, compare, consequence

Your attack strength S meets the defender’s revealed claim D (the defender may add one Tactic / reserve). Then:

OutcomeConsequence
S > DYou take the node. The rival’s claim is discredited (removed); yours stands as the new garrison. Excess S−D is lost. The loop-back perk fires; a →Field trigger may chain on.
S = DBoth claims are discredited; the node falls empty. The chain ends.
S < DYour paper is discredited (Findings lost); the rival’s claim stands; the defender gains 1 Standing. The chain ends.

Nothing here is violence — a discredited claim is a reputation scooped, not a piece destroyed.

Tactics & the bluff

Tactics are one-shot cards, held hidden and played at the moment named on them — a Feint to swell a claim on reveal, an Ambush (Prior Publication) to discredit a challenger before the compare. Because a Lodged claim’s strength is concealed, every attacker gambles: over-commit and waste findings, or under-commit and be repelled.

First in the FieldThe Clock · Winning

The Exhibition Clock

A shared clock counts down to the Grand Exhibition. Crucially, the players drive it — every clash in the Hall brings the end nearer, so the aggressor sets the pace.

  • Advance the marker +1 each time a challenge is resolved in the Hall, and whenever a card says so (e.g. Sensational Discovery, Press Sensation).
  • Escalation: from clock space 5, every successful claim advances the marker an extra +1 — the season accelerates, claims come thick and fast, and a double-leader can suddenly reach for the third band.
  • The close: when the marker reaches CLOSE, finish the current round (so all have had equal turns); then the game ends.

The patient builder wants a long, quiet season; the sensationalist wants to slam it shut before the great engine matures. The Hall is the clock.

Determining the Leads

The three Lead medallions are always held by whoever currently best meets each condition, and pass the instant someone overtakes — check after every action.

Winning the Game

Outright — the knockout

At the start of any round, if one naturalist holds all three medallions, they win immediately. Because it is checked only at a round’s start, the table always gets one full round’s warning to break a two-medallion threat — so a knockout must be defended, not merely seized.

At the close

When the clock closes and the round is finished, the naturalist holding the most medallions wins. If tied, break by: most Standing, then largest collection, then most Findings banked.

⚔ Why nobody turtles

Ignore the Hall and you never tick the clock, never gain Standing, and slowly lose draft priority to the warlord who does — who, unchecked, simply takes a third band and wins outright. The cold engine must, sooner or later, present.

Factions & Tactics

The Factions

  • The Systematist — Findings never spoil; +1 strength on your first claim each round. (the long game)
  • The Sensationalist — each claim you contest ticks the clock +1; +1 str attacking a held node. (the aggressor)
  • The Polymath — once a turn ignore one toll; +1 Specimen per chain step. (the combo)
  • The Collector — +1 Specimen each time you activate a Field card; peek the row before drafting. (the drafter)
  • The Illustrator — once a round, first conversion is 1:1; Findings cap +1. (the producer)

The Tactics

  • A Sensational Specimen (Feint) — +2 to your side on reveal.
  • Prior Publication (Ambush) — discredit a challenger before the compare.
  • A Whole New Genus (Overrun) — excess Acclaim carries to an adjacent field.
  • Anonymous Referee — cancel a Tactic just revealed.
  • Pre-emptive Letter — when challenged, +1 defence per field you lead.
  • Press Sensation — +2 Standing; clock +1.
  • Borrowed Type Specimen — ignore the Hall toll once this turn.
  • Erratum — recover the Findings from a failed claim.

Iconography

Specimen  ·  Finding  ·  Acclaim (claim strength)  ·  Standing  ·  → Band = chain trigger  ·  wax seal = a card’s build cost.

Turn at a glance

  1. Take one action in one band (Field / Study / Hall).
  2. Resolve its Activate; Passives always on.
  3. Follow any Trigger — pay fuel + toll — and chain on.
  4. A repelled Hall step ends the chain.
  5. End: spoil Findings above cap; note throughput; pass priority.
  6. At each round start: reset priority by Standing; check the knockout.
